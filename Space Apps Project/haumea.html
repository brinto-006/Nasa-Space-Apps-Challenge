Haumea

About Haumea

Haumea is one of the fastest rotating large objects in the solar system, which gives it a unique elongated shape. It is located in the Kuiper Belt and has two known moons: Hiʻiaka and Namaka.

Haumea's rapid rotation, which takes just about four hours, causes it to have an elongated, rugby-ball-like shape. It is one of the fastest rotating bodies in our solar system.

Haumea has two known moons, Hiʻiaka and Namaka, both named after Hawaiian goddesses. Hiʻiaka is the larger of the two and was discovered in 2005.

The surface of Haumea is believed to be covered in crystalline water ice, which makes it one of the brightest objects in the Kuiper Belt. The presence of ice suggests that Haumea may have undergone a collision in the past.
