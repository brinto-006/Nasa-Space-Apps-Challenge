About Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to harbor life. It has a diverse climate and biosphere, making it suitable for millions of different species.

Earth's surface is 71% water, consisting of oceans, lakes, and rivers. The rest is covered with continents and islands. The crust is composed primarily of oxygen, silicon, and aluminum.

Earth's atmosphere is composed of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), with trace amounts of other gases. It provides the necessary oxygen for animals and protects the planet from harmful solar radiation.

The average temperature on Earth is about 15°C (59°F), but it varies significantly depending on location, altitude, and season.

Earth has a strong magnetic field generated by its molten iron core. This magnetic field deflects solar wind and protects life from harmful cosmic rays.
