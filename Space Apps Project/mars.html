Mars

About Mars

Mars is often called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish appearance. It has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, and a massive canyon system called Valles Marineris.

Mars's surface is composed primarily of iron oxide, giving it a reddish color. The surface is covered with dry riverbeds, craters, and large volcanic plains.

The atmosphere of Mars is very thin, composed mostly of carbon dioxide. The thin atmosphere provides little protection from solar radiation and allows heat to escape easily.

Temperatures on Mars range from -125°C (-195°F) near the poles during winter to 20°C (70°F) at the equator during summer. The average temperature is about -60°C (-80°F).

There are polar ice caps made of water and frozen carbon dioxide. Recent discoveries suggest that there may be liquid water beneath the Martian surface, which is crucial in the search for potential microbial life.
