Eris

About Eris

Eris is the most massive known dwarf planet in our solar system and is even larger than Pluto. Its discovery in 2005 led to the reclassification of Pluto as a dwarf planet.

Eris is thought to have a surface covered in frozen methane, which gives it a bright, reflective appearance. Its highly reflective surface indicates a mix of ice and rock.

Eris has one known moon named Dysnomia. It was discovered in 2005 and is used to measure Eris's mass.

Eris was discovered by Mike Brown and his team in 2005. Its discovery prompted the debate that eventually led to Pluto's reclassification as a dwarf planet.
