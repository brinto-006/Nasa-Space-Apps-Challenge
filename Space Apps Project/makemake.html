Makemake

About Makemake

Makemake is one of the largest known objects in the Kuiper Belt and was named after the Rapa Nui god of fertility. It has no known moons and is known for its bright surface.

Makemake's surface is covered with methane, ethane, and possibly nitrogen ice. Its bright surface makes it the second-brightest object in the Kuiper Belt, after Pluto.

Unlike Pluto, Makemake is thought to have only a tenuous atmosphere, if any at all, composed mainly of nitrogen and methane. It is believed to lose much of its atmosphere when it moves farther from the Sun.

Makemake was discovered in 2005 by a team of astronomers led by Mike Brown. It was officially recognized as a dwarf planet in 2008.
