Jupiter

About Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. It is known for its Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth, and its many moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system.

Jupiter's atmosphere is composed mostly of hydrogen and helium. The planet's rapid rotation causes its clouds to form into bands, and it experiences some of the fastest winds in the solar system.

The Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging for at least 400 years. It is larger than Earth and is one of Jupiter's most recognizable features.

Jupiter has 79 known moons. Four of these, known as the Galilean moons (Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto), are large enough to be seen from Earth with a small telescope.

Jupiter has a strong magnetic field that is 20,000 times stronger than Earth's. It has a vast magnetosphere that extends millions of kilometers from the planet.
