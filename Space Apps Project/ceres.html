About Ceres

Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter. It is the only dwarf planet located in the inner solar system and is believed to have water beneath its surface.

Ceres's surface is composed of a mixture of water ice and various minerals, including hydrated minerals such as carbonates and clay. It also has bright spots that are believed to be salt deposits.

Scientists believe that Ceres has a significant amount of water ice beneath its crust. This makes it a candidate for potential habitability, at least for microbial life.

Ceres was discovered in 1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi and was initially classified as a planet. It was reclassified as an asteroid and then as a dwarf planet in 2006.
