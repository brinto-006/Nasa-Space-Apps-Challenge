Mercury

About Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system and the closest to the Sun. It takes just 88 days for Mercury to complete one orbit around the Sun, making its year the shortest of all the planets. Mercury has almost no atmosphere, resulting in extreme temperature fluctuations.

Mercury's surface resembles that of our Moon, covered with craters from billions of years of impact. It is composed mainly of iron and has a large iron core that makes up about 85% of its radius.

Mercury has an extremely thin atmosphere composed of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium. This thin exosphere cannot retain heat, leading to drastic temperature changes between day and night.

Daytime temperatures on Mercury can reach up to 430°C (800°F), while at night, temperatures drop to -180°C (-290°F). This makes Mercury one of the most extreme environments in the solar system.

Mercury has a magnetic field, but it is about 1% as strong as Earth's. Scientists believe that its weak magnetic field is due to its partially molten core.
