Uranus

About Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and has a unique axial tilt of about 98 degrees, which makes it rotate on its side. It has a pale blue color due to the presence of methane in its atmosphere.

Uranus has the most extreme axial tilt of all the planets, essentially rolling on its side. This tilt gives rise to extreme seasonal variations, with each pole getting around 42 years of continuous sunlight or darkness at a time.

Uranus's atmosphere is made up primarily of hydrogen and helium, with a significant amount of methane, which absorbs red light and gives the planet its cyan color.

Uranus has 27 known moons, named after characters from the works of Shakespeare and Alexander Pope. It also has a faint ring system, discovered in 1977, consisting of dark, narrow rings.

Uranus has an unusual magnetic field that is tilted at 59 degrees from its axis of rotation. Unlike other planets, Uranus's magnetic field does not originate from its core, but from somewhere closer to its surface.
