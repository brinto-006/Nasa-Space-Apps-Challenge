Neptune

About Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun. It is known for its vivid blue color, caused by the methane in its atmosphere. Neptune is a dynamic planet with the strongest winds in the solar system.

Neptune's atmosphere is composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane. The methane absorbs red light, giving the planet its deep blue color. The atmosphere also has extremely fast winds, reaching up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).

Neptune's Great Dark Spot is similar to Jupiter's Great Red Spot—a massive storm system. These storms form and dissipate over time and are thought to be powered by Neptune's internal heat.

Neptune has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest. Triton is unique because it has a retrograde orbit, meaning it orbits Neptune in the opposite direction of the planet's rotation.

Neptune's magnetic field is tilted relative to its rotation axis and is offset from the planet's center, similar to Uranus. It is believed to be generated by electric currents in its water-ammonia ocean layer.
